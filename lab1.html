Founder-Level Strategic Worksheet

Entrepreneur Mastery Academy

Section 1: Opportunity Depth Analysis

1. Problem Intensity Audit

Describe the core problem, its financial and emotional impact, and why it remains unsolved.

2. Market Validation

What proof exists that demand is real? Summarize customer conversations and insights.

Section 2: Value & Competitive Positioning

3. Competitive Mapping

List competitors, their strengths and weaknesses, and identify market gaps.

4. Unique Value Proposition

Complete: We help ______ achieve ______ without ______.

Section 3: Business Model Engineering

5. Revenue Architecture

Define pricing model, lifetime value, acquisition cost, margins, and break-even point.

6. Scalability Test

How will your business scale efficiently? What breaks at 10x demand?

Section 4: Risk & Strategic Defense

7. Risk Matrix

Identify operational, financial, market, legal, and reputation risks.

8. Worst-Case Scenario Planning

If your venture failed in 18 months, why would it happen?

Section 5: Founder Psychology

9. Founder Weakness Audit

Be honest about your skill gaps and behavioral limits.

10. Discipline & Systems

Describe your productivity, review, and learning systems.

Section 6: Strategic Vision

11. 3-Year Vision

Define measurable targets and positioning goals.

12. 12-Month Execution Plan

Break the year into 4 focused quarters.

Section 7: Ethical Foundation

13. Ethics & Responsibility

Define non-negotiables and long-term trust principles.

Final Founder Challenge

Investor Pitch Outline

Outline your 5-minute pitch covering problem, solution, market, model, traction, advantage, projections, and ask.

“AI can organize information. It cannot build resilience. It cannot take risks. It cannot face rejection. That responsibility belongs to you.”

Complete this worksheet with independent thinking. Your strategic clarity is your competitive advantage.
